PERFECTION: THE ENEMY OF PRODUCTIVITY

  • Mythri Shivakumar
  • Class of 2022

Chasing Perfection

For a better part of our lives, we’ve been conditioned into thinking that we need to strive to achieve perfection or everything would be of no use. Often it is misinterpreted as one believes that there is a certain benchmark to reach perfection. What we fail to realize is that everyone has a different perception of perfection. It is not standardized and neither can it be standardized. It is an illusion we set for ourselves to gain control and has it been positively impactful? This is debatable. It is no secret that across the world, in any stream of work, team-work is super essential. It is what binds any project together. And when this whole idea of “perfection” enters the team, a little shake in the boat is evident. At the same time, it gives rise to competition. As long as there is healthy competition, it is beneficial for the entire team. They reap the most profit from these friendly bouts.

The real problem arises when this takes a turn down the toxic alley. It almost feels like a person trying to overshadow the other. It continues to drive a wedge in the team and the whole purpose is lost. When you break it down, you see how deeply ingrained this concept of perfection is in a human mind. While it is alright to put in your best at everything you do, striving for ultimate excellence can prove to be the enemy of productivity.

Ultimate Independency

This sense of overcontrol forbids people from carrying out menial tasks. This chase after perfection restricts one to depend on others. Delegating work is an easy way to get work done. But sometimes, when you are obsessed with the idea of faultlessness, you tend to try and get all the work done by yourself. And how well will it bode with productivity? There is a reason a team is placed. In order to meet deadlines and get work done. But once you take over the bulk of the work or whole of the work, not only are you overexerting yourself there is no certainty that you will get all the work done, anyway. In short, this is like trust issues. You do not trust anyone else to deliver other than yourself and while it may seem ideal in the beginning, in the long run, it is just draining and futile.

Progress is a concept forgotten on the path to perfection.

Self-Sabotage

God forbid you do not hit the 100% mark, you are beyond out of control. When you strive for nothing but excellence, you are subconsciously pushing yourself to deliver not just 100 but more than 100 of what you can give. While it is an amazing mode of self-motivation when you underdeliver, you become your worst critic and that is not healthy. This is when your professional life starts interfering with your personal life and slowly, you start losing a part of you and it just keeps diminishing your morale further. Flexibility, which helps your balance your life, is lost. It is a downward spiral from there. From thereon forth, you either bite off more than you can chew or just confine yourself under this false pretense of excellence and forget about everything else. In this case, you are messing not only with the productivity but also your mental health which is a total no go.

These are certain things that striving for impeccability has resulted in. While slowly there the concept of perfection being perception is gaining popularity, it is still tough to say it has been totally eradicated. Because while it has its own set of cons, it does motivate others. How healthy is motivation is the question here. If it begins interfering with the productivity, you need to take a step back and recalculate everything because what is perfection if you do not meet the set brief. And if it begins messing with the balance of your professional and personal life, then there is more than just recalculating all the steps that led you to that point. Do your best but ensure your best doesn’t result in the worst outcome.
