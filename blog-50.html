A Present to yourself - Being alone

  • Prerana Rangdal
  • Class of 2023

The moment we enter into gatherings, surroundings point out the imperfections. In these situations, we feel insecure about ourselves. Right from our childhood, everyone wants perfect grades, perfect looks, etc. The human brain has an inherent tendency to look for perfection at all times. It is hardwired to do so, as this drives us to find the best solutions. But it also drains our energy and mental capacity. A common misconception about perfection is that it must be perfect in all aspects of life. This myth has been deeply ingrained in our society for decades, but we are not asking people to stop striving for perfection; rather, we are asking that it is okay if they do not.

If we can accept flaws in our daily lives, we will see that there is no need for perfection. Some things are better off when they are not perfect. Let us accept the imperfection to improve our lives and relationships with people around us! When we try to live a life with perfectionism, it is never enough. We end up feeling like we have been chasing something that is not tangible. It is important to acknowledge that it is okay not to be perfect in our daily lives. Therefore, when we wake up in the morning, let us not start our day with a checklist of what needs to be done or an unrealistic goal for the day. Instead, do the best you can at this moment.

"It's all about being true to your inner self."

Being alone feels like waging a war without arms. But confidence is said to be the strongest arm one has ever got. Be confident enough to give yourself the most precious gift of preferring your own company. Being alone doesn’t mean you have to shut out the world around you. But know that you choose yourself over people around you. It’s okay to feel you have no company to talk to, share stuff, etc. It’s fine when you see your friends hanging out but you are all alone making a presentation or an assignment or binge-watching. It’s alright when you feel like hanging out with someone but you have no one.

Being alone is all about prioritizing yourself over others. You may choose career over enjoyment, yet it’s okay to want to enjoy. You should also know that you can accompany yourself. Having bad company might be a hundred times more destructive than being alone.

When you are alone, you don’t give others the power to hurt you. Self-hurt can be healed but scars given by others are going to be permanent ones. It’s okay to be alone even on special days like festivals, birthdays, promotion days, etc. You can have a party by yourself. I agree that it’s good to have company sometimes and seeing someone else can make you happy at times. But you are very special, you are capable enough to accompany yourself.

Being alone doesn’t mean you fall in the genre of introverts, you can be outgoing, loving, yet all by yourself. When you are alone, you discover a lot about yourself. You become a good observer and listener. You become independent, strong, confident, and self-sufficient. You become the best version of yourself. You don’t fear anyone or the fear of being judged will not overtake you. You will learn to accept who you are and get comfortable in your skin. Most of all, you will start loving yourself.

There are cases where people get depressed because they are alone. When life gives you lemons, you need to make lemonade. We all have to survive the path of life alone. If someone has given up on you, you can always find someone better than them. But, if you choose to be alone, you also have to learn to get along with it. There will be days where you will feel low and distorted. It takes courage to stand alone and birds flying in a flock will never get it. Being alone is an experience in itself. Let us enjoy it and start vibing by ourselves.
