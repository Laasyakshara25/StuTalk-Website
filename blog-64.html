NBA - More than a Sport

  • K. Revanth
  • Class of 2025

The National Basketball Association, founded in the year 1946, is a major professional basketball league in North America. Just like IPL in India, it has a crazy fandom all around the globe. A game of basketball in the NBA consists of 48 minutes with a division of 4 quarters, with each quarter being 12 minutes. This league has 30 teams consisting of young men from the United States of America and Canada competing to win the championship. This league has its teams divided into two parts, the Eastern and Western conferences. Each conference has its finals and the winners of the conferences compete for the ultimate cup. These teams pick different university players through a process called the NBA draft. The draft is held every year where these teams select the players who’ve given the best performance at the university level and get a chance to go to the professional level.

Out of the 30 teams, some of the famous teams are Chicago Bulls, Los Angeles Lakers, Boston Celtics and Golden State Warriors. These teams are known for their best performance in the league. They had some of the best players of the era like Michael Jordan, Kobe Bryant, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Magic Johnson, Lebron James, Stephen Curry, and many more. My personal favourite is Michael Jordan who played for the team Chicago Bulls from the year 1984 to 1998. Jordan won six championships during the period 1990 to 1998. His dedication to the game and determination for victory is what made him my favourite.

“Some people want it to happen, some wish it would happen, others make it happen”- Michael Jordan.

Why is it more than a sport for me? Well, my love for NBA and basketball started when I was in the third standard when I saw a few players playing this game. I saw them dribbling the ball, stealing the ball, passing the ball fantastically and scoring a basket. Gradually I developed an interest in the game and started playing it. I had many beautiful experiences and memories with this sport. It made me who I am today and helped me deal with the ups and downs in life. My favourite moment that I cherish the most about the game was during the eighth standard when I wanted “23” as my jersey number as it was Jordan’s. Even one of my teammates wanted to have it so the issue got bigger and we had to shoot free throws. Whoever scored the highest out of 10 free throws would win the number. My teammate scored 5/10 and I scored 9/10. The last throw of this game was epic as I shot an airball just to show him that I won. This was one of the most iconic moments, not just related to basketball, but in my life.
