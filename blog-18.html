THE SNOW ADOBE OF INIDA

  • KOWSHIKA
  • Class of 2023

An unblemished land with awesomeness overloaded, "Manali" is a true bliss for those who wish to get close to the magical snowy hills. The thrill of winter adventure attracts every explorer’s adrenaline urge. Guess what? There is a story behind the name too! Derived from the combination of Manu-Alay, Manali lies in the North of Kullu valley. This valley is often referred to as the “Valley of Gods”.

My unexpected love affair with Manali, “India’s summer queen”, started when I'm on my way to Manali from Shimla. On the way to Manali, surprisingly there was a sudden snowfall, seeing the whiteout from the sky, and on the other side of the road, the Beas river flowed in all glory. I felt like I was entering a wonderland. The next day from my room’s balcony, I could look at the ubiquitous snow-capped Himalayas surrounding Manali, it was such a pleasant morning.

If you are someone like me, who likes adventure sports like rafting, skiing, paragliding, zorbing, trekking, and camping. Then you should visit the Solang valley. It is a splendid valley in Solang village which offers magnificent views of glaciers and snow-capped mountains. It is the peak point that gave me the hilarious experience which I haven't expected while commencing my journey. Above all, I personally liked the river-rafting in the Beas river. This has given me a memorable adventure and a wonderful experience.

It is well said that, Manali is the true paradise of India.

Vashisht temple and Hidimba temple are the most prominent tourist attractions in Manali. The Vashisht temple is decorated with excellent and beautiful carvings on wood. This temple is famous for its sulfurous hot water springs. The water from this spring is believed to have great healing powers, and it is also believed that one dip in this water spring is enough to relax the body and mind. And next comes the famous place of Tibetan Monastery. The main attraction of the Monastery is the handicrafts of Tibetan artistry and the carpets that are woven and sold in the Monastery. The statue of the Lord Buddha presides in the Monastery.

The indigenous tribal population in Manali wear their traditional attire. Usually spun out of wool, the colorful and attractive apparels reflect the culture of the local people of the area. The people there are so disciplined that they don’t throw the waste on the roads however we tourists are spoiling it by littering the beautiful and clean roads of Manali.

What to do next? Yes, it’s shopping!! The most exciting part of the journey is when you walk around the city to find outstanding stuff that you can take back home to remember all the memories even years after the trip. Manali’s Mall Road is quite popular and is as fascinating as it gets, you can stroll at the Mall Road and shop for some local delights. Not just that, you can also treat yourself to some amazing cafes. If you are a food lover like me, you can have delicious food in the streets of Mall road. My favorite dish in Manali is Himachali Khatta, this recipe is both sweet and sour in taste and a must-try when you visit Manali.

Manali is indeed a land of many options. It guarantees that everyone finds something for themselves. It will surely disconnect you from your daily busy schedule. Also, picture yourself being pampered in the arms of mother nature. All of it will add a whole new shade to your Manali trip. All in all, it was a good experience, to sum up the overall experience, Manali will never let down your expectations. It will remain an experience as good as visiting a cold country for the Indians.
