Dawn of The Zombie Botnets

  • Anvika Muthineni
  • Class of 2022

Cyber-attacks have been on a steady rise in the last few years. The armies of infected devices that carry out these attacks and cause damage are called 'botnets'. Interestingly, most of the computers used in these attacks are owned by common people but taken over by hackers. Over the years, botnet armies have been getting bigger, smarter and more destructive

Any electronic device that connects to the internet is vulnerable. It just has to be able to run a type of computer program called a ‘malware’, which turns computers into zombies and then forces those devices to do whatever the hackers tell them to do. The only goal of this malware is to steal as much of money and data as it can from big companies while not getting detected as long as possible

To successfully build a zombie army of smart devices, a hacker writes a computer program that searches the internet for connected devices. Then, the program breaks into that device and tries to guess the password so that it can install a malware. Unfortunately, guessing a password is easier than most of us can imagine. New devices like smart TVs, wi-fi routers and security cameras are sold with a default password. Usually it’s something easy like “password”. A survey conducted by a computer magazine deduced that more than one-third of people never change their passwords. And many of them use the same password for all their devices, which is also risky.

The hacked devices can make the army even bigger as the malware might direct the device to search the internet for other potential computers to infect. The biggest botnets can take over millions of computers at a time

Surprisingly, the users do not know that their devices have been compromised as the malware makes sure to do its work stealthily.

Many botnets can stay hidden for months or even years in the host computers.
In the future, these attacks will get worse because botmasters (botnet controllers) are finding more ways to send data from individual devices through phishing.

Preventing botnet malware is just like preventing any other type of malware and because it is very difficult to remove once it is in the system, prevention is our only real defense.

1.Regular software updates are vital. Be sure to keep your operating system and applications up to date.

2.Think before you click. Do not open mail attachments from senders you don’t recognize. Similarly, be wary of any email asking you to click on a link.

3.Use a quality Anti-Malware software to keep threats at bay.

The unlimited power of a botnet can give cyber criminals endless possibilities to scam individuals and businesses. The users should make sure that their computers aren't involved in doing cyber criminals dirty work by taking the necessary precautions!
