CHASE YOUR INSECURITIES

  • Nitya Jahnavi
  • Class of 2023

The moment we enter into gatherings, surroundings point out the imperfections. In these situations, we feel insecure about ourselves. Right from our childhood, everyone wants perfect grades, perfect looks, etc. The human brain has an inherent tendency to look for perfection at all times. It is hardwired to do so, as this drives us to find the best solutions. But it also drains our energy and mental capacity. A common misconception about perfection is that it must be perfect in all aspects of life. This myth has been deeply ingrained in our society for decades, but we are not asking people to stop striving for perfection; rather, we are asking that it is okay if they do not.

If we can accept flaws in our daily lives, we will see that there is no need for perfection. Some things are better off when they are not perfect. Let us accept the imperfection to improve our lives and relationships with people around us! When we try to live a life with perfectionism, it is never enough. We end up feeling like we have been chasing something that is not tangible. It is important to acknowledge that it is okay not to be perfect in our daily lives. Therefore, when we wake up in the morning, let us not start our day with a checklist of what needs to be done or an unrealistic goal for the day. Instead, do the best you can at this moment.

"Part of living a happy life is realizing that flaws are not only unavoidable but also necessary for happiness."

Accepting imperfection in your life is necessary to be mentally healthy. There are many ways to do this, one of the most important ways is practicing self-compassion. It can be done by avoiding comparisons with others and understanding that it’s not about having flaws but accepting them. It is also about forgiving yourself for your shortcomings and flaws. Many people don't realize how much they rely on perfectionism in their lives, which can lead to high levels of anxiety and depression. Practicing acceptance of imperfections in daily life will help you avoid these negative consequences on your mental state.

Here is a little message to the people who are feeling insecure!
“I think that you are beautiful. I think that you are beautiful in the way you hold yourself against all odds. Your eyes gleam with self-love and affirmation, despite the questioning glare of others, despite hurdles, both high and low, despite the mental-emotional turmoil you often find yourself in, and the disordered nature of life in all its shades. I think that you are beautiful when you do not feel beautiful. When you look at yourself in the mirror and notice the tiny imperfections that adorn you like jewels, the blemishes, and the scars and all your tender curves and hard edges, and your lips arch up into thin smiles that do not reach your eyes. You may not feel beautiful or perfect or enough, but that is when beauty engulfs you the most.”
