A REWIND: 2020

  • Dharanidhar Soma
  • Class of 2023

The year 2020 was devastating as the chaos started with the death of KOBE BRYANT, the Greatest NBA player in history. Later the coronavirus has hit and caused a human catastrophe greater than the Spanish Flu that occurred way back in the year 1920. The virus started in Wuhan city of China as an epidemic that ended up as a pandemic as the human contact affected over more than 170 countries worldwide. Over 1 Billion people have been infected with the virus worldwide among which 50 million people ended up dead because of the virus.

Over more than 325 million schools have been shut down worldwide. Human resistance and finance were put to test. Restaurants, parks, public places were shut down. Professional sports got canceled. Many lost their jobs worldwide. Wearing masks, sanitizing, social distancing, and work from home have become a part of the new normal. Zoom and other platforms have become the medium for communication. We demonstrated creativity by being our own barbers, bakers, and apart from it, fear, tension, pain, anxiety, and boredom were the common emotions that were reported all over the world. Researchers, doctors have put in their best efforts to make a vaccine for the virus but the efforts went in vain.

Apart from the virus, riot’s due to racial injustice for the blacks have shaken the world. The death of GEORGE FLOYD in the United States ignited the movement. Blacks from all over the world united and fought for their rights. The BLACK LIVES MATTER movement was heard and fought from NEW YORK to LONDON and from AMSTERDAM to SYDNEY.

Being resilient and adopting made us get through the year.

The Influencers like Shawn Mendes, MR Beast have put their best efforts to entertain us while we were at home. First, there was fear, pain, and struggle at the beginning of the pandemic. Later, we ended up being creative, supportive, and being persuasive in fulfilling our passion.

2020 was the toughest year for some, dealing with anxiety, pain, and stress. For others, it may be about being in solitude, staying productive, and experiencing the same world with their eyes from a different perspective. As we got through this year, let’s hope we get through these chaotic times as nothing is permanent, and let’s hope peace restores in the world.
