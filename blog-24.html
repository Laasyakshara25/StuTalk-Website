BULLYING

  • Nithya Kanakam
  • Class of 2023

Bullying is the abuse or mistreatment of a vulnerable person by a stronger or more powerful person. Listening to the word bullying, the first thing that pops into everyone’s mind is kids being bullied. But it is not just about children, being bullied as an adult is also a grave matter to be concerned about. Every person has been a victim of abusement at some point in their life. The most commonly reported types of bullying are verbal harassment, social harassment, and cyberbullying. Kids in their schools, students in their colleges, and employees in their offices, everyone is a victim of mistreatment at a given moment. Bullying is not a hitch, it is a major issue, which in turn can lead to mental trauma.

A bully mainly targets a person who is isolated, sensitive, and lacks confidence. Bullies not only hurt the victim verbally, but they are capable of physical assault too. Being bullied makes us feel lonely, scared, and hopeless. Social Anxiety and Depression are also the results of being bullied. There are some perilous effects of bullying like suicide, physical and emotional agony.

Every year 42% of the kids report about being bullied in their schools.

Overcoming the long-term effects of bullying might be one of the toughest things that one will ever have to do in their life. But, it is important to overcome that phase to move on happily in life.

Here are a few ways through which you can overcome bullying:
1. Ignore the bully: Never take a bully’s words to your heart. They are just doing it to make you feel low. They don't know who you really are, and how amazing you are. So never ever get upset by someone who doesn’t know your wonderful personality.
2. Talk to a counselor: Opening up to a counselor about something that’s disturbing will help alleviate your anxiety. Counseling helps you learn how to deal with a bully.
3. Speak up: The only way to fight back a bully is by speaking up. Reporting the bully to the higher authorities not only helps you but also benefits the other people who are victims of the same bully. This will give courage to many other victims to come forward and fight for themselves.
4. Take proper care of your health: Bullying can be stressful but taking care of your physical and mental health is most important. Follow a proper diet, eat healthy food. Meditation helps you bring down stress levels.
