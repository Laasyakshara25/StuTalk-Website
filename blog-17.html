FIGHT DEPRESSION

  • NITYA JAHNAVI
  • Class of 2023

There comes a time in life when you feel like everything is just shattering around you, and your words don't appear to be coming from your heart anymore. Your favorite hobbies aren't your favorites anymore. You feel that you are just hanging in there like a rock and everything seems to be a mess. You will have the craziest of mood swings.

You seem to be lost on everything. You feel like crying out loud, and you desperately want someone to hold you tight and just say that "everything is going to be okay!", truth be told, you may find no one. Read it again, because it's the truth and a sad and painful one at that. You are alone in this battle for the most part. You feel like something unknown is devouring your strength and stress is the worst part of it all.

You will possibly try to speak with your fellow mates, and when you couldn't speak about it, you just conclude that they are going to understand you. When you try to put your feelings down on a paper, words don't come out to explain what you feel. If they ever do come out, they don't justify your true feelings. You feel like something is disturbing you from inside. This seems to be the worst phase of your life.

You spend sleepless nights only to know what's wrong with you. You start searching for reasons to stay alive. Here comes the most crucial part of your journey, "The suicidal thoughts", you just sit in a corner of your room trying to figure a way out of this. Yet, you feel that "suicide" is the only option. But it isn't. If you are living in this world, then there is something you need to achieve from it. You may have even made a few attempts that were unsuccessful (it's a bliss that they're unsuccessful).

If you are brave enough to attempt suicide, then definitely you can win this world.

Somehow your brave heart manages to stay alive. You feel like staying alone, far away from people. Now all you want is peace. You are all fighting with yourselves just to get through this difficult period. It's tough, I can't say for how long you need to go through this, but one day, you will emerge as a winner.

Visiting a psychiatrist does help you a lot. It's okay to get therapy. People will talk about it. But, be mature enough to let their talking not affect you. It's your life, you can do whatever you want. But someone else's judgment can never decide who you are. Gather yourself together and remember, "You are not alone in this". Let's fight depression, I can promise you one thing, We will get through this with our head held high, we will rise and shine.
