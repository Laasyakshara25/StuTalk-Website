HOW I MADE A JOKE OUT OF MYSELF?

  • V. Vamshi Krishna
  • Class of 2022

“Epitome of cringe”, as I am fondly called by my best friend. Comedy came naturally to me and I know that's probably the understatement of the year. Ever since I was a kid I was the class clown and I guess that's why no one ever took me seriously. Growing up with strict parents, there was an immense amount of pressure on me to always be there to lighten up serious situations in my house on a daily basis. I am the kind of person who makes jokes at a funeral. Keep in mind that it all comes from a good place. I believe it is my way to vent, my way to express my feelings. Honestly, making such jokes has become my coping mechanism. So, without further ado, let’s see how I made a joke out of myself.

I always wanted to go on stage and humor people with my talent which I was not sure about. I always had a thing for stage performances, I tried singing, dancing, and other stuff and realized that I am terrible at them. Honestly, I thank god that I'm not a singer because I would have been egged on a daily basis. I found my knack for comedy, my thunder in my 10th standard when I was asked to speak something for a few minutes at an event. I didn't know what to say so I started cracking jokes. The response was not so good but I was happy that I was able to put up a few smiles. From then on, my journey was like a rollercoaster ride. I used to go to these random open mics and was able to improve myself. To be honest, in the beginning, I used to crack open like a raw egg on the stage. There were times where none of my jokes used to work, but that's the beauty of it, you get to learn, improve and excel eventually. I started working on my sets and spending more time writing and letting it all out.

Life is so much easier with a sense of humour!

I started to do standup comedy in my own style which is just being as cringy as possible. I started having more fun writing the jokes than I had told them on stage. I used to spend hours laughing at my own jokes. I used my followers on Instagram as a test audience for my jokes, to see whether the world could handle my full potential. This helped me refine my jokes and make them more audience-friendly and eventually helped me avoid an FIR. Around this time, I learned a very valuable lesson that helped me a lot in my career as a self-proclaimed comedian, “you can not make everyone happy”, these five words keep me going every time I perform or every time one of my sets goes bad. It took me so long to realize that there are no “clean” jokes.

They are fun for a reason and you cannot be funny while maintaining borders and always looking over your shoulder. This made me unique from everyone else. I learned this from my favorite comedian and inspiration Daniel Sloss. Watching his shows is what helped me grow as a comedian and gave me my own style of comedy. I had many inspirations in this field like Kevin Hart, Adam Devine. Indian comics like Sahil Shah, Azeem Banatwala were like my idols. Whenever I was not doing good or I needed some advice I used to message them. They helped me clear my mind and gave me advice so that I won't make any mistakes which I'll regret. So this is my not so inspirational story and in the end.
