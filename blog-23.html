LET'S TAKE A VACATION FROM OUR BUSY LIVES

  • Shivani Varma
  • Class of 2023

People nowadays are too busy in their jobs and are very concerned about the material needs. However, in a bid to selfishly guard our time, we lose sight of some important aspects in life. Hectic work life and extreme pressure make people unmotivated and stressed. Rewinding, people were workaholics, always in a state of mind to prove themselves the best in the industry they work in. But for once, if you think about what’s going on around your life, ask yourself a question: “Are you really happy with your life, and also is it worthful?”. Of course, it is important to financially grow and be productive in life but we must also enjoy our lives and be happy too.

Besides work and our routine schedule, we need to take a break from being busy and realize that we are missing out on so many experiences in our lives and the best idea is to treat ourselves with a vacation and come back fresh to our work. Let me tell you some reasons on why a vacation is important:

It is a good remedy for stress, anxiety, and also depression. It also improves mental and physical health. Reduce your stress, whether you are experiencing a burnout or are embattled with lots of pressure from work, going on a vacation will dramatically decrease whatever stress you are experiencing. However, in modern society, chronic stress can be destructive to our bodies. Getting away for regular vacations and leaving our everyday troubles to give ourselves a break from the constant high levels of these hormones and a chance to repair some of the damage.

Travelling is a crucial part of life, you'll be in contact with nature, the sun, the ocean, or just the fresh air.

You become productive, studies agree that if you don’t take vacations, your productivity suffers. Long periods of work without any break lead to irritability, poor decision making, weak communication and relationships, and more days off due to ill health. When you go on a vacation, you reverse all those negative side-effects and come back with greater focus, an optimistic mindset, and a sore in productivity. Getting away from work can be good for you, as the sun's rays are essential (in moderation) for the skin and your body in general while breathing in fresh air is also great, whether you're in a forest surrounded by wildlife or you’re in a park in the city!

You should strengthen your social life, while working, you tend to compromise on your relationships, especially within your family. As work deadlines take priority, you miss out on family gatherings and regular bonding opportunities. You cannot compensate for lost time and opportunities by waiting for retirement. Make up for lack of quantity by increasing quality and intensity when you take some time off. Vacations are your ideal investment as travelling together is the best way to build relationships. Improve physical and mental health. Stress can contribute to heart diseases, high blood pressure. It can also be a major contributing factor to anxiety and depression. So, taking a vacation will lessen the risk. Therefore, I can only say that, don't make time only to work. Make it more productive by going on a vacation from your busy life and enjoy it to the fullest.
