Filter

  • Yashwanth Vuppula
  • Class of 2024

The google definition of ‘Filter’ goes as “pass(a liquid, gas, light, or sound) through a device to remove unwanted material.” How filters are supposed to be used: Filter Chai, Filter Coffee, Filter Water. But, what we use filters for: filter our beautiful faces, hide our insecurities, show this world a fake face.

Upload a smile and write a gentle reminder for myself to not add any filters to my picture again. There's so much filter in the picture that if you see the original, either you'll laugh or you'll get scared.

Be Original, Be Yourself!

In the second case, upload a non-filter picture and write, “Now, this is some filter-free real face.” Am I not attractive? Who cares?

Our generation misunderstood what a filter is and how to use it. It's okay to add some filters to make pictures look good but adding many filters will only harm and not otherwise. Ever heard of selfie dysmorphia? Well, let me make things simple for you.

Have you ever clicked your selfie with a filter on Snapchat and liked it very much and you clicked the same selfie again without any Snapchat filter and hated your face.

Well, you got used to filtering your appearance so much that you forgot your original face. People who don't like their original faces and are addicted to filters are unconfident. According to a survey, many youngsters commit suicide and others undergo surgeries for a better facial appearance. They fall for an unrealistic artificial face that they want to show to the world. But, they don't realize that filters aren't real.

You can apply makeup in real life but are you going to apply it for the rest of your life? If yes, then you are living in an illusion. Get out of it.

Remember, you are beautiful even without any filter. All the praises that you get in the real world are for your real face not filtered appearance.
