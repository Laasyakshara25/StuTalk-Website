The Next Step

  • Kondam Supriya
  • Class of 2023

Life is nothing less of a roller coaster ride. If the past two years have taught me anything, then it is that life is the most uncertain thing in the world. While this has been my lesson, it’s not the same for everyone. Not for those extended family members and neighbors who ask me what my “next step” is going to be.

Yes, I heard that question at least a dozen times in the past week and I’m not even half past my graduation. I’m still a long way to get that certificate in my hand and people are already worried about the next chapter of my life. While I’m still trying to figure out an offline life routine, I understand that the expectations of the next step are quite high.

When the expectations are high along with the pressure, I’m not sure what would make me happy or help me make the best choice.

There are a lot of options to choose from which are definitely not helping. While your parents secretly wish you to do something, your siblings expect you to do something else, and so does your friends, family, well-wishers, cousins, and everyone, though their intentions may be pure, they simply overrule what you truly want, with the reason “You are too young to make the right choice” and make the decision for you!

While this part stays aside, one of your friends is already preparing to write some entrance tests and to your surprise, some may have already done it. While one plans to take over the family business, the other works hard to land a job, while some are already interning with start-ups, getting the exposure.

Giving it a long thought would only make my head burn and a heavy intake of caffeine would only make some improvement. However, with the experience of the past two years where lives crumbled more than ever, I know being “happy” is my priority. Enjoying the little moments in my college life would make me happy. Not worrying about the future when you are just in the second or third year of college
