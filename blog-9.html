Love: A Perspective

  • Revanth Thoutam
  • Class of 2023

Have you ever wondered what love is? Does it even exist or is it just another illusion that we humans have created? Is love just an amalgamation of sweet words and actions? For a mother, it is the care and concern for her child above all others. For a father, it is the responsibility that he holds for his family. Everyone has a diverse meaning and definition of love. But isn’t it the very first thing that brought us all into this beautiful world?

‘Love’, the word itself makes one sense magic. Science calls it a chemical reaction in the brain. Humans, as souls, call it a celestial bliss. Love is a force, an edge on which the world strives to live upon. Love is not only in happiness or kind words or gentle actions people show each other.

It is in the pain of a mother while birthing her child and the pride of a father when his child prospers in life. It exists in the fights that siblings have and it is the zeal that makes you work relentlessly to reach your goal. The affection and loyalty that your friends have for you and the trust that your pet has in you.

The very existence of everything around us would not have been possible if it weren’t for love.

It's in happiness where little things mean a lot to you. Love for power is the reason humans waged wars against each other. And it was the love for peace which ended these wars. As we see, it’s love for knowledge and development that makes humankind more civilized day after day.

It’s the only force that every entity in nature is gifted with, as it’s not bound by species, race, religion, culture, and gender. The same love for yourself boosts the journey of your life. It’s the only feeling that can be felt in every emotion and thought that one can express.

My parting thoughts would be, love is a blank which every living being fills with their perspective to give their world a meaning. It is an abstract force with distinct directions but equal magnitude. A treasure that is quite different for each hunter but the same reason that attracts them. I believe that it is a perception that’s a riddle with unique answers which gives the purpose of life with many other lives interconnected.
