COVID-19 AND ONLINE CLASSES

  • Bekkam Praneetha Singh
  • Class of 2024

COVID-19 has forced Universities across India and the world to suspend physical classrooms and shift to online classes. There have also been debates on the nature of classes, and the future of examination and evaluation — whether they could be conducted online or not. While faculty grapples with new ways of managing this sudden transition to online education, students are left clinging to their mobile phones and computer screens.
There are quite a few advantages and disadvantages that are worth considering. Adjusting to the new way of learning and the existing schedule is one of the hectic tasks not only for students, but for teachers too. It allows students doing part-time jobs to study without compromising on their work hours and to spend time with their families.

Can 'Online Classes' be the future of education?
Yes, definitely. In my view, online classes can be the future of education because this pandemic has changed our lives in all aspects. Not only the share market, way of living standards, transport, industries, and businesses are affected along with students' lives. Then came the "Online classes", the only option we had to teach young minds. Not only you but no one expected that life would take this turn. Everything changed, the poor were unhappy like always but this time, the rich were too.

Virtual classes, the new normal has indulged in our lifestyle and our way of learning too.

The concept of "Online classes" was not new to the people who studied online through some apps. But, for the others, learning online was totally new. This is hard not only for students but for teachers too. Because they need to struggle a bit more to prepare and to explain topics. If at all this pandemic prolongs, online classes will be the only option to teach students. So, first of all, it can change the way of learning and teaching. This is something like a teacher should speak to the students through their systems and vice versa. This is weird because this covid has not only made things weird but people too.

I don't mean that these computers would replace teachers, but it can be a part of the teaching-learning process. I don't know until when these so-called "Online classes" would continue but, I can surely say that this would be a new way of education in the years to come. Long gone are the days where knowledge has been acquired in the classrooms. We are all in the situation where traditional Zoom or Google meet apps are used to acquire the same knowledge.

Though, there are many benefits when learning online, I truly believe that there would be no student-teacher interaction and this can affect personal understanding capability. Server problems and poor internet connectivity can somehow break the flow and interrupt studies. Online classes can affect students' eyesight, as excessive use of devices is very harmful to the eyes. It also affects their mental and physical growth. As the students are using a system or phone constantly, listening to the classes without any physical activity can bring health issues in the days to come. Moreover, students are easily attracted to violent or adult content, especially in their teens. In this "Internet" world, parental control is important. If parents neglect their duty, then the students’ life would turn in an unexpected direction. To some extent, I feel like one day all the students will wish only for "Online Classes" but in the end, it all depends on the individual on a personal level.
