OBSSESSIVE - COMPULSIVE DISORDER

  • Sahiti Varma
  • Class of 2023

A person who likes to be clean is depicted his/her positive characteristics. You feel better and comfortable when things around you are organized. When it comes to common people keeping their house yard car neat and tidy makes them feel at ease. But how much cleaning is too much cleaning?

Yes, here I am presenting you about this very known issue that is obsessive compulsive disorder aka OCD. We often come across people who deal with anxiety, every minor incident makes them feel anxious and people who suffer from OCD are more likely to deal with obsessive cleaning. You read it right, people are dealing with over cleaning disorder. You might wonder what’s wrong with cleaning too much. There’s nothing wrong with cleaning way too much unless it affects your mental health. People with OCD feel they have to do their rituals to prevent some dreaded catastrophe.

A You might also get it wrong when I said people who like things to be organized and clean to feel at ease they aren’t terms as people with OCD, they are usually neat freaks who like things to be in place because that makes them feel energetic and which might also help them in being productive throughout the day.``Neat freaks'' do not have an obsession like people with OCD do. OCD is based on fear & anxiety and them doing their chores is the response to their obsessions. Do all OCD sufferers have this obsession with cleaning? No, only a fraction of them experience the obsession with neatness and cleanliness

Therapy will aid you to reduce 80% of your OCD.

Effect of pandemic on the masses:
The safety measures imposed by the government like social distancing, quarantine, and self-isolation represented a stressful lifestyle that caused the fear of getting infected. Among the vast variety of obsessions, the fear of dirt and excessive washing were the most common ones which affected about 50% of covid-19 patients. The situation was worse for people dealing with OCD. Although the government took necessary measures to keep the public at peace.

How to treat and reduce the anxiety caused by OCD:
1) Meditation: Meditate at least two times a day this reduces anxiety and makes you feel less stressed.
2) Set an agenda: Make a plan to do things in order, set a time table in which a certain period goes only for cleaning, and not a minute less or more should be appreciated.
3) Medication: Consult a doctor, there’s nothing wrong with going for therapy.
4) Try to be negligent ( try not to care about the mess).
5) Write down your obsessions and create a worrying time in your agenda. It helps you to the core.
